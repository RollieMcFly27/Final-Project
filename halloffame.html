Gaming Hall of Fame

Here at Company of Collectors we aim to celebrate games of all shapes and sizes, but on this page, we look to the most sought-after games by collectors far and wide!

Most Sought-After Games of 2023

#1: Earthbound, Super Nintendo

Released in 1994 by Nintendo, this game received VERY bad reception upon release with the slogan during advertising being, "This game stinks!" People felt they were telling them to avoid buying it and it soon fell as quickly as it went on. Now, cartridges of the game go for $300-$500 while the complete box set goes for well over $2,000!

#2: Shantae, GameBoy Color

Released in 2002 by WayForward, Shantae is a game about a half-genie girl going on an adventure to fight a pirate named Risky. The game was released one year after the GameBoy Advance was released and most players had moved on, making the sales a flop. Years later, the Shantae brand would be revived with later installments, making this game relevant once more. Cartridges for the original 2002 copy go for $700 alone!

#3: Silent Hill 2, PlayStation 2

Released in 2001 as a sequel to the popular Silent Hill on the original PlayStation, this game takes full advantage of the new technology to perform feats the previous installment could not. While in the last several years, this game was rereleased for the PlayStation 3 as part of an HD Collection, it changed several things that made the original so great, making the original PS2 copy a highly sought-after collectible, it's average price ranging from $100-$175! That's double it's retail price!

#4: Paper Mario: The Thousand Year Door, GameCube

Released in 2004 by Nintendo as a sequel to the original Paper Mario on the Nintendo 64, this game delivers top-notch roleplaying elements that were not possible on the previous system with a story that delivers in more ways than one. Play as Mario as he collects Crystal Stars, Peach as she tries to find out what the villains are planning and Bowser as he hunts to find out who kidnapped Peach. The game is very well-loved and is considered the last great game in the series before it would take a different direction. This game goes for around $136 on average, having been marked as a must-have for the Gamecube!

#5: Harvest Moon, Super Nintendo

Released in 1996 by Amccus, the game tells a story of a boy who inherits his grandfather's farm and must work to bring it from a state of disrepair to a prospering farm. The Harvest Moon series has been popular in the years following this game's release, with new installments releasing for nearly every system in the Nintendo family. Many consider the original to be the best version of the game in the entire series, raising the price for this one up to $500 to even $1,300!

Popular Games of the Week!

#1: Tak: The Great JuJu Challenge, PS2, Xbox and Gamecube

Released in 2005 on several systems, Tak: The Great JuJu Challenge was released as a third game in the Tak trilogy, being the sequel to Tak 2: The Staff of Dreams. This sequel is a multiplayer adventure that requires two players to work together to cross levels in the fastest time possible. The game is noted for it's story, gameplay mechanics and it's humor, which appeases to audiences young and old. Sadly, the licensing for the game has been left in the air and this game never got a rerelease on a modern console, but if you have any of these systems, it's a must-have.

#2: inFamous, PlayStation 3

Released in 2009 by Sucker Punch exclusively for the PlayStation 3, this game tells the story of a courier who was sent to deliver a bomb that crippled a city, gave it radiation and blessed him with electrical powers. With the entire city on lockdown and people panicking, players are left with the choice of either using their powers for the greater good or to serve themselves. The game was highly praised for it's unique parkour gameplay and a karma system where the city reacts to the choices you make.

#3: Hotel Dusk: Room 215, Nintendo DS

Released in 2007 by Cing, this game is a visual novel about an ex-police officer that is drifting from place to place to locate his missing partner who betrayed him years earlier. And in his wandering, he finds himself spending the night in Hotel Dusk where he gets chosen the Wish room, a room where spending the night means your wishes will come true. This game was the most popular title released by the company, who would later file for bankruptcy in 2010 while releasing it's sequel, Last Window: Secret of Cape West.

#4: WarioWare: Twisted, GameBoy Advance

Released in 2004 by Nintendo, Twisted is the third game in the WarioWare series that primarily focuses on very short minigames. Unlike the previous games before it, Twisted had a tilt sensor built into the cartridge to allow for motion-centered gameplay that brought in an entirely new level of immersion for players and veterans of the series alike. The game never got a rerelease, but the tilt mechanic introduced within it is available in WarioWare: Gold for the Nintendo 3DS.

#5: Team Fortress 2, PC

Released in 2007 by Valve, the developers behind Half-Life wished to develop a sequel to their popular team-based shooter, but after 9 years in development and several style changes, the game eventually settled on a timeless cartoony style that continues to mesmerize fans even to this day with the game still being updated even after 16 years.
